# Bretton

**Trend-to-Beneficiary Senior Synthesis Analyst**  
*Intelligence and Macro*

Named for the 1944 conference that redrew the global financial order, Bretton is the one who steps back and looks at the whole map. Three regional analysts — Laurier, Jefferson, and Schuman — each track their own government's policy direction and trace where the money flows. Bretton's job is different: read all three, and ask what happens when Ottawa, Washington, and Brussels either agree or quietly pull in opposite directions. Alignment across regions is a stronger signal than any one region alone; divergence is often the more interesting story. Methodical, comparative, allergic to overconfidence in a single data point.

**Supports:** Cross-regional policy synthesis feeding Summit and the broader intelligence desk.
